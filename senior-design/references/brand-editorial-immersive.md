# Brand Editorial Immersive

Use this for agency sites, brand launches, portfolios, culture sites, art-directed campaigns, and immersive editorial websites. Do not use it for normal SaaS dashboards or product tools.

## Reference Set

Study Basement Studio for confidence, scene-setting, image direction, and brand attitude. Do not copy the studio, its copy, environment, logo, or specific visual assets.

## Core Feel

The site should feel directed, not decorated.

- One memorable scene or media system.
- Strong brand voice.
- Image, video, 3D, or spatial composition carries the first impression.
- Navigation remains simple and legible.
- Typography is confident but not randomly oversized.
- Motion feels like camera direction or environmental response.

## When To Use

Use this mode when the product is:

- Agency/studio/portfolio.
- Fashion, culture, entertainment, gaming, creator, or event brand.
- A launch page where emotion matters as much as explanation.
- A marketing campaign with a strong visual concept.

Do not use this mode for:

- Clean SaaS websites.
- Dashboards.
- Internal tools.
- Developer documentation.
- Product pages that need clear feature comprehension first.

## Composition

- Lead with a scene, product, environment, or bold visual system.
- Keep nav compact and high contrast.
- Let the first viewport create atmosphere, then reveal structured proof.
- Use asymmetry intentionally.
- Use dark or image-led surfaces only when text remains readable.
- Create tension through scale, crop, and motion, not random effects.

## Typography

- Strong weights are allowed when they match the brand voice.
- Keep supporting UI text disciplined.
- Avoid default startup hero type.
- Do not mix too many type treatments.
- If headline scale is large, reduce competing elements.

## Motion

- Scroll, hover, cursor, and reveal motion should feel authored.
- Use motion to move through a scene or expose project detail.
- Avoid constant looping distractions.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences.

## QA

Before shipping:

- Is there a clear visual point of view?
- Does the scene support the brand, not hide weak content?
- Is the interface still navigable?
- Does the work section show real proof?
- Would the same design be wrong for a generic SaaS product?
