# Light Product SaaS

Use this for clean white or neutral SaaS pages, product marketing, onboarding-style sites, screenshot-led software pages, and calm B2B product interfaces.

## Core Feel

- White, off-white, or soft gray foundation.
- Restrained accent color, usually blue, black, green, red, or warm orange.
- Clear software screenshots or UI mockups as proof.
- Compact, readable headline scale.
- Calm spacing with strong alignment.
- Minimal decoration; product surfaces carry the visual interest.

Avoid empty startup minimalism where the page has no product evidence.

## Page Patterns

- Split hero: concise promise plus product screenshot or workflow panel.
- Centered hero: short headline, form/CTA, screenshot below.
- Product proof: screenshot, feature callouts, and short captions.
- Trust section: logos, customer quotes, integration strip, or workflow proof.
- Feature blocks: 2-4 concrete product capabilities, not generic benefits.
- Onboarding section: steps, checklist, imported data, or setup flow.

## Product Screenshot Rules

- Screenshots should show believable UI chrome, data, empty states, or controls.
- Use stable frames with modest radii and subtle shadow.
- Keep screenshots readable; do not over-compress important text.
- Match screenshot density to product type: tools can be denser than marketing pages.
- Do not use fake dashboards unless the product is actually data-driven.

## Typography

- Default desktop hero range: `44-68px`.
- Default section heading range: `30-48px`.
- Body copy should be practical and specific.
- Use sans-serif for product clarity unless the brand demands editorial type.
- Keep labels, tabs, and button text intentionally sized.

## Color And Material

- Use neutral surfaces before adding color.
- Use accent color for CTA, active state, focus, or important data.
- Gradients should feel like light, not wallpaper.
- Borders should separate surfaces without making everything look boxed.

## QA

- Is the product visible early?
- Does the page explain what the software does?
- Are screenshots believable and useful?
- Are headings restrained and readable?
- Is the page polished without generic card spam?
