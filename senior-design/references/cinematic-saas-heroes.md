# Cinematic SaaS Heroes

Use this only when the user explicitly asks for cinematic SaaS hero sections, optical gradients, finance/wellness/commerce product pages, abstract landing pages, or large product-overlay compositions. For normal SaaS, developer tools, dashboards, and product websites, prefer `clean-product-websites.md` or `product-dashboard-ui.md`.

## Core Feel

The page should feel like a premium product film still with usable UI layered into it.

- Dark or muted foundation with a large optical field.
- Centered or lower-left hero copy with tight hierarchy.
- Soft gradient, bokeh, iridescent, lens, or atmospheric material.
- Crisp UI, navigation, and CTAs sitting above the atmosphere.
- Product mockup or app screenshot anchored below the headline or into the lower viewport.
- Minimal copy; the visual carries emotion and category.

Avoid random gradient wallpaper. The background must support positioning, product category, or product proof.

This is not the default SaaS mode. Do not use atmosphere to compensate for weak product proof.

## Visual Patterns

- Finance tracker: dark top, soft multicolor lower glow, centered headline, glass CTA, small reassurance text.
- Wellness/finance intersection: black canvas, giant iridescent arc, lower-left copy, small pill CTA.
- B2B copilot: pale blue glass field, subtle construction lines, centered hero, large translucent product UI rising from below.
- Data/product overlay: product screenshot appears as a real interface, not a decorative card.

## Hierarchy

- Navigation stays small and quiet.
- Brand mark sits top-left; CTA or login actions sit top-right.
- Hero headline is the dominant element, but restrained.
- Supporting copy sits directly under headline and should be short.
- Primary CTA is a pill or glass button, centered or aligned to copy.
- Secondary proof text is small and close to the CTA.
- Large visuals should never create dead empty gaps.

## Typography

- Prefer clean geometric or humanist sans-serif for finance, commerce, and wellness SaaS.
- Use strong weight contrast rather than oversized type.
- Desktop hero headlines usually sit around `44-64px`; use `64-72px` only when the layout has enough room and the user asked for a cinematic feel.
- Mobile hero headlines usually sit around `30-40px`.
- Keep most weights between `400-560`; do not make the entire page bold.
- Keep line-height tight but readable.
- Avoid decorative fonts; the visual material provides the personality.

## Backgrounds

Good background materials:

- Soft blurred color fields.
- Iridescent arcs or lens-like refractions.
- Subtle grain/noise.
- Thin construction lines or circular guides.
- Desaturated product-tinted gradients.
- Dark tactile material when paired with hardware or health context.

Rules:

- Keep the text zone calm enough for contrast.
- Let color collect at edges or lower half when headline sits center.
- Use overlays, masks, or darkening layers to protect readability.
- Avoid full-screen rainbow intensity.

## Product Overlay

- Product UI should be legible enough to signal real functionality.
- Glass product overlays need low-contrast borders, subtle blur, and real controls.
- Place large product UI below the hero copy when it is the proof.
- Crop product UI intentionally; do not show an entire screenshot if a partial interface feels more cinematic.
- Keep UI chrome crisp against atmospheric backgrounds.

## CTA Treatment

- Pill CTAs work well in this pattern.
- Glass CTAs need clear border and readable text.
- Primary CTA should be obvious but not shout.
- Tiny reassurance text, like trial terms, belongs close below the CTA.

## QA

- Does the background feel specific, not stock?
- Is the text readable over the optical material?
- Is the hierarchy clear within 3 seconds?
- Is the product visible or implied convincingly?
- Are headlines controlled, not poster-sized?
- Does the hero avoid dead space while staying cinematic?
